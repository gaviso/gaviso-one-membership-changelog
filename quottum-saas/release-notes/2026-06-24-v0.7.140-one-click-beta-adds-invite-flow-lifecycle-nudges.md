## Operator console — one-click beta tester adds
- **Path A — email already on Quottum:** insert as **approved immediately**; welcome email goes out on the spot. No separate Approve click after Add.
- **Path B — email not on Quottum:** seamlessly routes into the new **invite flow** (14-day single-use link). Recipient lands on `/beta-signup` with email locked, completes the intake form, and on submit the workspace activates immediately on `beta_access` (no operator review needed). A "Pending invites" strip with Revoke shows up on `/admin/beta`.

## Janitor + lifecycle nudges (in-platform cron)
- **Daily janitor** (`08:00 UTC`) flips expired beta invites to `expired` so the same email can be re-invited without manual cleanup.
- **Daily lifecycle nudges** (`08:10 UTC`) send up to two one-shot check-ins per user, deduped forever via `lifecycle_nudges`:
  - `tester.inactive.14d` — approved beta tester, no sign-in in 14 days
  - `workspace.inactive.30d` — Free-plan workspace, no quotes/contracts/clients ever, workspace older than 7 days
- Both nudges end with *"Reply to opt out of these check-ins."*
- Scheduled via **pg_cron + pg_net** with the cron secret pulled from Supabase Vault — no external scheduler dependency.

## Privacy / DPA implications
- The clickwrap log added in v0.7.135 now also captures a **content hash** of the doc the user accepted (fetched from `quottum.io/version.json`). Closes the proof loop for legal disputes.
